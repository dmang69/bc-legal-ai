# BC-Specific Procedural Glossary

Expanded procedural terminology for BC Supreme Court and Provincial Court
practice.

---

## Court Structure

**Provincial Court of BC** — the trial court of first instance for most
criminal matters, small claims (up to $35,000), and family (non-Supreme
Court) matters.

**Supreme Court of BC** — the superior trial court, with inherent
jurisdiction. Hears civil actions (unlimited monetary), family, judicial
reviews, and appeals from Provincial Court in some circumstances.

**Court of Appeal for BC** — appellate court from BC Supreme Court and
some tribunals.

**Supreme Court of Canada** — the final court of appeal, on leave granted.

---

## BC Supreme Court Civil Rules — Selected Terms

**Notice of Civil Claim (Form 1)** — originates an ordinary action.

**Response to Civil Claim (Form 2)** — the defendant's pleading.

**Counterclaim (Form 3)** — a claim by a defendant against a plaintiff.

**Third Party Notice (Form 5)** — a claim by a defendant against a
third party.

**Petition (Form 66)** — originates a petition proceeding, including
judicial reviews.

**Response to Petition (Form 67)** — the respondent's pleading in a
petition proceeding.

**Requisition (Form 17)** — used to request an order that is available
without a hearing, or to schedule a chambers matter.

**Notice of Application (Form 32)** — filed to bring an application on
notice.

**Application Response (Form 33)** — response to a Form 32 application.

**Affidavit (Form 109)** — the standard affidavit form.

**Certificate of Pending Litigation (CPL)** — a notice registered against
title to land indicating litigation affects the property.

## BC-Specific Timelines (Selected)

Verify current versions of the Rules — timelines are amended periodically.

**Judicial review under JRPA** — 60 days from the decision under review.

**Response to petition** — generally 21 days after service (7 days if
served in BC).

**Application response** — generally 5 business days before the hearing.

**Written submissions on petition** — set by the court or the Rules;
often 21 days for petitioner's, 21 days for respondent's, 7 days for
petitioner's reply.

**Filing of chambers record** — typically 4 business days before hearing.

**Notice of appeal to BCCA** — generally 30 days from the order under
appeal.

Always check the current rule text and any recent amendments.

## Chambers Practice

**Chambers** — a proceeding before a judge (or associate judge) in a
setting less formal than trial, for applications and petitions.

**Chambers list** — the list of matters scheduled to be heard in
chambers on a given day.

**Long chambers / short chambers** — long chambers matters exceed a
default duration (varies by registry) and are scheduled separately.

**Chambers record** — the collected materials for a chambers matter,
prepared and filed in advance.

**Chambers brief** — sometimes used interchangeably with chambers
record; in some usages, refers specifically to the argument.

**Speaking to the record** — presenting oral argument on a chambers
matter, typically time-limited.

---

## Associate Judge (formerly "Master") Jurisdiction

**Associate Judge** — a judicial officer of the BC Supreme Court with
jurisdiction over specified matters, primarily interlocutory. Cannot
hear the merits of most petitions or trials.

**Registrar** — a judicial officer with jurisdiction over specified
administrative and interlocutory matters, including cost assessments.

Consult the BC Supreme Court Civil Rules for the current allocation of
jurisdiction — this has been subject to change.

---

## Costs

**Costs** — the recoverable expenses of litigation, typically awarded to
the successful party.

**Costs on Scale B (or "ordinary costs")** — the standard scale of
costs in BC Supreme Court, calculated by reference to tariff units in
Appendix B of the Rules.

**Special costs** — costs awarded on a substantial-indemnity basis for
misconduct or in exceptional circumstances. Substantially higher than
Scale B.

**Costs in the cause** — costs to be paid by the ultimately unsuccessful
party at the end of the proceeding.

**No order as to costs** — each party bears their own costs of the
particular matter.

**Costs thrown away** — costs incurred that have been wasted (e.g. by an
adjournment for which one party is responsible).

**Bill of costs** — the itemized statement filed to have costs assessed
by the registrar.

---

## Judgments and Orders

**Judgment** — the final determination of a proceeding on its merits.

**Order** — any direction of the court, whether interlocutory or final.

**Consent order** — an order made on the consent of the parties.

**Default judgment** — a judgment obtained where the defendant has
failed to file a response within the required time.

**Summary judgment** — a judgment made without a full trial, where the
material facts are not genuinely in dispute.

**Perfected order** — an order that has been entered by the registry
after being signed and filed.

**Enforceability** — an order is enforceable once entered; some orders
may require a further step (e.g. writ of execution) to enforce.

---

## Appeals

**Appeal as of right** — an appeal that can be brought without leave.

**Appeal with leave** — an appeal that requires the permission of the
court.

**Leave to appeal** — an application for permission to appeal.

**Interlocutory appeal** — an appeal from an intermediate order, before
final disposition.

**Appeal book** — the compiled record on appeal, containing the
pleadings, judgments, and relevant materials.

**Notice of appeal** — the document commencing an appeal, filed within
the time limit prescribed by the rules.

**Standard of review on appeal** — for questions of law, correctness;
for questions of fact, palpable and overriding error; for questions of
mixed fact and law, generally palpable and overriding error unless an
extricable question of law is identified (see *Housen v Nikolaisen*
2002 SCC).

---

## Disclosure and Discovery

**Discovery** — the pre-trial process by which parties exchange
information about the case.

**List of documents** — a document listing the records in a party's
possession or control relevant to the matters in issue.

**Document production** — providing the documents on the list to the
other party.

**Examination for discovery** — the pre-trial oral examination of a
party under oath.

**Interrogatories** — written questions answered under oath. Less
commonly used in BC than in some jurisdictions.

**Privilege log** — a list of documents withheld on a claim of
privilege.

**Redaction** — the striking out of privileged or irrelevant portions of
an otherwise producible document.
