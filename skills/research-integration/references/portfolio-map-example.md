## Cross-Domain Concepts Visible on the Map

Looking across the columns, several concepts appear in more than one:

**Capability / Authority Structure**
- IntentOS (capability tokens)
- Quantum-QuadOS (eBPF policy enforcement)
- AGI Governance (regulatory authority)
- BC JR analogy (legal standing)

**Signal / Signature / Measurement**
- Echo Calculus (Σ quintuple)
- Geomagnetic Reversal (paleomagnetic signals)
- AI-Native Internet (mesh telemetry)

**Ontological Categories**
- Energy Regimes (thorium vs uranium ontology)
- Quantum Consciousness (Orch-OR ontology)
- Reality Desync (simulation ontology)

Each cross-domain concept is a candidate for concept lattice entry
(Module 2) and possibly for a synthesis chapter (Module 8).

## What the Map Reveals

Three observations that would not be visible from any single thesis:

**1. The Physics column is broadest.**
This might reflect the true center of gravity of the research program, or
it might reflect legacy — several works published without a unifying
frame. Worth asking: is Physics the arc, or is the arc "measurement /
authority / ontology" across domains?

**2. The Life Sciences column is thinnest.**
Two works, both currently active. Either this is a genuinely narrow
interest (fine — not every column needs to be broad) or the two works are
underdeveloped complements that could reinforce each other. Worth checking
Module 6 (complementarity detection).

**3. The Policy column bridges Computing and Physics.**
AGI Governance ties to IntentOS (both concern authority structure). RoD
ties to Reality Desync (both concern epistemic infrastructure). These
bridges suggest the Policy column is the SYNTHESIS layer of the portfolio,
not just an application layer.

If the Policy column is the synthesis layer, that has publication
implications — the policy works may be the natural venues for citing the
broadest cross-portfolio work.

---

## What the Map Should Prompt

Every time you refresh the map, ask:

1. Are there works I forgot to include? (Older projects, side works, drafts)
2. Are there domains that have grown enough to deserve their own column?
3. Are there works that no longer fit the program? (Candidates for closure)
4. Is there a work missing that would obviously fit? (Candidate for next project)
5. Is the arc visible in the map, or hidden?

The map is a diagnostic. Its main value is what it makes visible that you
already knew but hadn't seen at once.
