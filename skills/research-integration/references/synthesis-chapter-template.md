# Synthesis Chapter Template

Full walkthrough for writing a chapter (or short paper) that pulls
together threads across multiple works in your portfolio.

---

## When to Write a Synthesis Chapter

Write one when:
- Three or more works share a concept the reader would miss without it
- A signature move in your thinking appears across works but has never
  been named
- You want a "flagship" work that establishes the arc of the portfolio
- Your citation graph is starting to be dense enough that a hub work
  would benefit the whole portfolio

---

## Structure

### 1. The Common Thread (1-2 pages)

Open with the connection. State the concept, principle, or method that
appears across the works being synthesized.

```
Across [Works X, Y, and Z], one move recurs: [description of the shared move].
In each work, it plays a slightly different role — in X, it [role]; in Y,
it [role]; in Z, it [role]. This chapter names the move explicitly and
develops it as a unified concept.
```

### 2. The Individual Contributions (2-3 pages)

Brief summary of each work's specific contribution to the shared theme.

For each work:
- One paragraph: what the work does
- One paragraph: how the shared move shows up in it
- One paragraph: what would be lost from this work without the move

### 3. The Emergent Framework (3-5 pages)

The heart of the chapter. What do the works together show that none alone does?

This is where you make the argument that the concept, principle, or method
is more than the sum of its appearances. Options:

- A formalization: define the concept precisely for the first time
- A typology: catalog the ways the concept appears
- A theorem: prove something about the concept that only becomes visible
  across works
- A framework: give the concept a name and a set of associated tools

### 4. Cross-Work Consistency (1-2 pages)

Show that the works actually agree on the concept — or, where they
diverge, name the divergence and explain it.

```
                Work X          Work Y          Work Z
Definition:     [same]          [same]          [same or diff]
Role:           [description]   [description]   [description]
Test:           [same]          [same]          [different]
Application:    [domain]        [domain]        [domain]
```

Where the works diverge, address it directly. Divergence isn't a problem
if you can explain it as domain-appropriate specialization.

### 5. Open Questions (1-2 pages)

What does the synthesis reveal as still open? Every good synthesis
generates new questions.

- Questions the concept raises but doesn't answer
- Extensions to domains not yet covered
- Limitations of the current framework
- Empirical tests that would strengthen or challenge the framework

### 6. Directions for Future Integration (1 page)

The forward-looking section.

- Next works that would extend the framework
- Combinations with other concepts in the portfolio (see concept lattice)
- Publications this synthesis makes possible

## Why the Synthesis Chapter Is Often the Best Work

The individual works make individual contributions. The synthesis
chapter makes the CONNECTION visible — and connections are frequently
more citable, more influential, and more distinctive than any single
contribution.

Three reasons:

**1. Rarity.** Most researchers don't do this work. Nobody else has
your whole portfolio in view. You are the only person who can write
your synthesis.

**2. Visibility.** A synthesis chapter is discoverable independent of
its constituent works. Someone reading about the concept can find your
synthesis without having found any of the source works first.

**3. Positioning.** The synthesis chapter positions you as the person
who thinks in terms of the concept. Not just someone who has worked in
several domains, but someone who has developed a signature move.

## Common Failure Modes

**Failure Mode 1 — Just a Table of Contents**
The synthesis chapter reads as "I did X, and I did Y, and I did Z."
This is a portfolio summary, not a synthesis. Fix: focus on the SHARED
move, not the individual works.

**Failure Mode 2 — Forced Unity**
The chapter claims a synthesis that isn't really there. Fix: if the
synthesis feels forced, don't publish it. Publish it when the connection
is real.

**Failure Mode 3 — Over-Formalization**
The synthesis adds so much formal structure that the original works
become unrecognizable inside it. Fix: keep the synthesis at the level
where the shared move is visible, not deeper.

**Failure Mode 4 — Under-Positioning**
The synthesis introduces the concept without saying it's a signature
move of your work. Fix: own it. The synthesis chapter is where you claim
the concept.

## Length and Placement

- As a chapter: 15-25 pages
- As a short paper: 8-12 pages
- As a preface or afterword to a book: 5-10 pages

Placement matters. If it's a chapter in a longer work, place it near the
front (to frame everything after) or near the end (to pull everything
before together). Middle placement dilutes it.
