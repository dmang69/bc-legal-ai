  intentkernel-arch: Formal treatment of same model with capability calculus
  quantum-quados:   The enforcement mechanism operationalizing capabilities
  BC JR analogy:    Illustrative — used to teach standing structure

  Formal definition is consistent between intentkernel-os and -arch
  Quantum-QuadOS uses the same definition operationally
  BC JR uses ANALOGICALLY not identically — this must remain clear

Cross-citation opportunities:
  quantum-quados should cite intentkernel-arch for the formal definition
  intentkernel-os should cite quantum-quados as the reference implementation

Publications this concept could unify:

## What Lattice Entries Reveal

Once you have 5-10 entries, patterns become visible:

1. **Which concepts are load-bearing across the whole portfolio.** These
   are your signature ideas. Own them explicitly.

2. **Which works are the "hubs".** Works cited by multiple lattice entries
   are hubs; they carry disproportionate weight.

3. **Which works are "underdeveloped for their potential".** Works that
   appear in many lattice entries but only briefly could be expanded to
   become hubs.

4. **Which cross-citations are missing.** Every lattice entry surfaces
   cross-citation opportunities. Log them and act.

5. **Which publication opportunities are visible.** The last line of each
   entry ("Publications this concept could unify") is a running list of
   next papers.

The lattice is a research strategy document disguised as an index.
