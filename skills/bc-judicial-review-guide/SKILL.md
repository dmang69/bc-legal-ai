# BC Judicial Review Guide

> **Disclaimer:** Legal information only. Verify *Judicial Review Procedure Act*, *Administrative Tribunals Act* (if applicable), Supreme Court Civil Rules, and limitation periods on BC Laws / Court websites.  
> **`[INDEPENDENT COUNSEL RECOMMENDED]`** for most JR and stay applications.

## Prerequisites handoff (from RTB / tribunal)

Before drafting, obtain:

1. Full tribunal record (decision, evidence filed, hearing notes if available)  
2. Grounds matrix (evidence ignored, fairness, jurisdiction, unreasonableness, fettering, *res judicata*, retaliation, illegal charges, etc.)  
3. Complete timeline of tribunal proceedings  
4. List of evidence ignored or mischaracterized  
5. Internal review / reconsideration path and outcome (e.g. RTA review provisions — **`[VERIFY section numbers]`**)  

## When to escalate

- Decision unreasonable, procedurally unfair, or jurisdictionally wrong  
- Internal review denied or upholds a flawed decision  
- Need for a **stay** of an order of possession or similar while litigating  

## Grounds framing (working list)

| Ground | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Procedural fairness | No notice; no chance to respond; interpreter denied; bias |
| Jurisdictional error | Outside statutory power; wrong Act applied |
| Unreasonableness | Outcome/reasoning lacks rational chain (Vavilov — verify pinpoints on CanLII) |
| Error of law | Wrong legal test |
| Fettering | Rigid policy applied without fact-specific exercise of discretion |
| Failure to consider material evidence | Key exhibits ignored |
| *Res judicata* / issue estoppel mishandled | Prior final decision on same issue |

## Stay applications (working framework)

Canadian courts often use a three-part interlocutory stay/injunction framework associated with ***RJR-MacDonald Inc. v. Canada (Attorney General)***, [1994] 1 S.C.R. 311 (verify on CanLII):

1. Serious question to be tried  
2. Irreparable harm  
3. Balance of convenience  

**Housing context:** loss of home often supports irreparable harm analysis — still prove with evidence (order of possession dates, children, disability, shortage of housing).

## Petition / procedure (high level)

1. Confirm correct originating process under Supreme Court Civil Rules + JRPA.  
2. Style of cause, parties, decision-maker as respondent as required.  
3. Affidavit evidence limited as appropriate for JR (record-focused).  
4. Service rules — **fatal if missed**.  
5. Hearing set; possible applications to set aside stay.  

**Do not invent form numbers** — pull current forms from the BC Courts website.
